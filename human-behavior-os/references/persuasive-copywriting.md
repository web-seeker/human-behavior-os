# Persuasive Copywriting

## 0. Relationship to Parent

This reference inherits all 7 modules, the Master Formula, and quality standards from the parent Human Behavior OS skill. It adds copywriting-specific frameworks, formulas, and psychological triggers for text-based persuasion.

---

## Copywriting Behavior Framework

### The Persuasion Sequence

Every piece of persuasive copy follows this behavioral sequence:

```
Attention → Interest → Desire → Trust → Action
(AIDA + Trust)
```

| Stage | Module | Copy Task | Time Budget |
|-------|--------|----------|-------------|
| Attention | Attention System | Headline, subject line, hook | 1-3 seconds |
| Interest | Need System | Lead, problem statement | 5-30 seconds |
| Desire | Emotion System | Benefits, transformation, story | 30-120 seconds |
| Trust | Trust System | Proof, evidence, guarantees | Throughout |
| Action | Decision System | CTA, urgency, next step | Final moment |

## Module-Specific Copywriting Applications

### Attention System → Headline & Hook Writing

**Headline Formulas by Attention Trigger:**

| Trigger | Formula | Template |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Curiosity | The [Number] [Surprising Thing] About [Topic] | "The 3 things your doctor won't tell you about sleep" |
| Benefit | How to [Achieve Result] Without [Pain Point] | "How to double your sales without spending more on ads" |
| Danger | [Number] [Mistakes] That Are [Negative Consequence] | "7 pricing mistakes that are killing your profits" |
| Result | From [Bad State] to [Good State] in [Timeframe] | "From burned out to thriving in 30 days" |
| Story | How [Person] [Achieved Result] by [Method] | "How a solo founder grew to $1M ARR with zero marketing spend" |
| Novelty | Introducing the [First/Only/New] [Thing] That [Benefit] | "The first AI that writes emails that actually sound like you" |
| Contrast | Why [Popular Approach] Fails (And What Works Instead) | "Why traditional networking fails (and what works instead)" |
| Secret | The [Hidden/Untold] Truth About [Topic] | "The untold truth about productivity apps" |

**Subject Line Formulas (Email):**

| Type | Formula | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Curiosity gap | "I thought you should know..." | "I thought you should know what happened at 3am" |
| Benefit + urgency | "[Benefit] — [Time limit]" | "Your free trial expires tonight" |
| Personal | "[Name], [question]" | "Sarah, are you making this mistake?" |
| Story tease | "[Character] did [unexpected thing]" | "A junior dev just outperformed the entire team" |
| Number + promise | "[Number] ways to [benefit]" | "9 ways to cut your meeting time in half" |

### Need System → Copy That Connects

**Need-Based Copy Angles:**

| Need Layer | Copy Angle | Voice & Tone | Example |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| L1 Survival | Problem-solution, urgency | Direct, serious | "Don't lose your data. Back up now." |
| L2 Efficiency | Time/money saved, simplicity | Clear, practical | "Cut your workflow in half. Seriously." |
| L3 Emotion | Feeling transformation, relief | Warm, empathetic | "Finally, a workspace that feels like home." |
| L4 Identity | Status, expertise, taste | Confident, aspirational | "For professionals who demand excellence." |
| L5 Growth | Potential, mastery, freedom | Inspiring, visionary | "Unlock what you're truly capable of." |

**The "So What?" Test:**
After every sentence, ask "So what?" If there's no clear answer tied to a user need, remove or rewrite it.

### Emotion System → Emotional Copy Engineering

**Emotional Copy Techniques:**

| Technique | How It Works | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Future pacing | Make them feel the future state now | "Imagine waking up to 50 new leads every morning" |
| Pain amplification | Describe the current pain vividly | "You know that Sunday night dread? That's your body telling you something." |
| Contrast framing | Before vs. after | "Before: 60-hour weeks. After: 40-hour weeks with better results." |
| Social proof emotion | Others' emotional reactions | "Join 10,000+ marketers who finally sleep well" |
| Specificity | Precise numbers create believability | "Increased conversion by 34.7% in 14 days" |
| Sensory language | Engage multiple senses | "Feel the difference when your inbox hits zero" |

**The Emotional Arc of a Sales Page:**

```
1. Hook: "Are you [struggling with X]?" (Empathy)
2. Agitate: "Here's what it's really costing you..." (Pain)
3. Reveal: "What if there was a better way?" (Hope)
4. Prove: "Here's how it works + proof" (Confidence)
5. Transform: "Imagine your life after..." (Aspiration)
6. Risk reversal: "Try it risk-free" (Relief)
7. CTA: "Start now" (Urgency + Anticipation)
```

### Trust System → Credibility Copy

**Trust Elements in Copy:**

| Element | Where to Place | Copy Technique |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| Social proof | After headline, near CTA | "Trusted by 10,000+ teams" |
| Authority | Early in copy | "Developed with researchers from MIT" |
| Specificity | Throughout | "34.7% improvement" not "huge improvement" |
| Transparency | Pricing section | "Here's exactly what you get and what it costs" |
| Guarantee | Near CTA | "30-day money-back guarantee, no questions asked" |
| Case study | Middle section | "How Company X achieved Y in Z days" |
| Anticipate objections | Before they arise | "You might be thinking..." sections |

**Objection-Handling Copy Pattern:**

```
Objection: "It's too expensive"
Response: "It costs less than your daily coffee. And unlike coffee, it pays for itself in the first week."
```

### Decision System → CTA & Conversion Copy

**CTA Formula:**

```
[Action Verb] + [Specific Outcome] + [Urgency/Scarcity if applicable]
```

| Weak CTA | Strong CTA | Why It Works |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| "Submit" | "Get Your Free Report" | Specific outcome |
| "Learn More" | "See How It Works (2 minutes)" | Low commitment + time bound |
| "Buy Now" | "Start Your Free 14-Day Trial" | Risk-free + specific |
| "Sign Up" | "Join 10,000+ Marketers" | Social proof + belonging |
| "Download" | "Get Instant Access" | Immediacy |

**Friction Reduction in Copy:**

| Friction | Copy Solution |
|----------|--------------|
| Too many steps | "Just 3 questions" / "Takes 30 seconds" |
| Uncertainty | "No credit card required" / "Cancel anytime" |
| Overwhelm | "We'll guide you step by step" |
| Privacy concern | "We never sell your data. Ever." |
| Commitment fear | "Try free for 14 days" |

### Spread System → Shareable Copy

**Copy That Gets Shared:**

| Share Motive | Copy Technique | Example |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| Self-expression | Opinion-forward, quotable | "The best time to plant a tree was 20 years ago. The second best time is now." |
| Helping others | Actionable, save-worthy | "10 things every new parent needs to know (save this)" |
| Identity | Expertise signal | "I've analyzed 1,000 landing pages. Here's what the top 1% do differently." |
| Emotional release | Emotionally resonant | "This made me cry and I'm not sorry about it" |

## Copy Templates

### Landing Page Structure

```markdown
# [Headline: Attention Trigger + Core Benefit]

[Subheadline: Clarify and amplify]

## The Problem
[Describe the pain — Need System + Emotion System]

## The Solution
[Your approach — unique, clear]

## How It Works
[3-step process — Decision System: reduce complexity]

## Proof
[Case studies, testimonials, data — Trust System]

## What You Get
[Specific deliverables — Need System: value clarity]

## Risk Reversal
[Guarantee — Trust System]

## CTA
[Action-oriented, low friction — Decision System]
```

### Email Campaign Structure

```markdown
**Email 1: Problem Awareness**
Subject: [Curiosity/Danger trigger]
Body: Agitate the pain, introduce the possibility of a solution

**Email 2: Solution Introduction**
Subject: [Benefit trigger]
Body: Present the solution, share a story/case study

**Email 3: Proof & Trust**
Subject: [Result/Social proof trigger]
Body: Evidence, testimonials, data

**Email 4: Urgency & CTA**
Subject: [Scarcity/Action trigger]
Body: Final push, risk reversal, clear CTA
```
